Welcome

Take a comfy seat, breathe, and let this be your little corner to reflect, notice patterns, and be gentle with yourself. You're right where you need to be.

How this space works

This app is here to help you notice what's happening, slow down, and choose responses that fit your values when someone with narcissistic patterns is part of your life. Nothing here replaces therapy or safety planning; it's a private place to track what you observe and how you want to move.

Your view ▾
Tap Your view in the header and pick the situation that fits you best. Behavior descriptions use plain language for your role (e.g. “your parent,” “your partner”) so you do not need to memorize abbreviations. You can change this anytime.
Log ▾
Use Log when something stands out. Add a short note if you want, set date and time, and choose the behavior that fits best—you're not grading yourself. Use Culturally influenced / ambiguous when background or intent might change how the moment reads. Save moment stores it. You can show or hide the Recent moments list under the form if you prefer less on screen. Logging still builds a factual, dated picture you can revisit in History without reliving everything at once. If a label you need isn’t on the Log tab, open Learn, find that behavior in the same category, and use Add to Log (or Restore to Log if you’d removed it from the shortcut list).
History ▾
Use History to search, filter by cultural flag, pick a from/to range, or add a summary when you want a wider picture. Good for noticing repeats and pacing yourself before you respond or set boundaries.
Learn ▾
Use Learn to read fuller descriptions, examples, and optional reminders about responses. Treat it as support for your clarity, not a script you must follow. When a behavior isn’t already on your Log shortcut list, open its entry here and use ＋ Add to Log (or ＋ Restore to Log when it applies).
Insights ▾
Insights summarizes how often patterns show up and lets you export or import data. Use Add custom behavior there if your situation needs a label that isn’t in the default list.
When your responses change ▾
When you set boundaries or act differently, a narcissistic parent or partner may push back with anger, guilt, or manipulation. This is normal and not your fault. You may feel conflicted, but over time, consistent self-care and reflection help you reclaim your emotional space. Journaling, support from trusted people, and self-compassion can make this process safer and empowering.
